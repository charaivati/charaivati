[SECTION: overview]
Initiatives are how users earn within Charaivati.
They live in the Earning tab of the Self layer.
An initiative is a user's economic activity —
their way of converting skills and drives into income.
[/SECTION]

[SECTION: types]
STORE: Physical or digital product sales
- User lists products, manages inventory
- GPS-enabled for local delivery tracking
- Example: Snacks by Arun, handmade goods

SERVICE: Skill-based service offering
- User offers time/skill for hire
- Portfolio, booking, reviews
- Example: Tutoring, design work, repairs

EDUCATION: Teaching and course creation
- User creates courses in their skill area
- Four course types: Skill/Sport, Academic,
  Art/Expression, Personal Growth
- Lesson videos auto-create feed posts

HELPING INITIATIVE: Community/social impact work
- Focus areas, objectives, awareness campaigns
- Impact tracking, donation support
- NPO-style structure within the platform
[/SECTION]

[SECTION: drive_to_initiative]
Drive types naturally align with initiative types:

Seeker → Education initiatives,
         research/writing services
Guardian → Helping initiatives,
           community service, security services
Builder → Store, service,
          any commercial initiative
Keeper → Service initiatives,
         craft stores, skilled trade services

AI should suggest initiative types based on
the user's drive when they ask about earning.
[/SECTION]

[SECTION: gps_module]
GPS is available for Store and Service initiatives.
It enables live location sharing during delivery
or field service.
Model: owner triggers a Live Session per order/event.
Link is shareable and updates every ~45 seconds.
The GPS feature is not the product —
the initiative owner's use of it becomes their product.
[/SECTION]

[SECTION: fund_location]
For users whose initiatives serve a social purpose,
or for the NPO layer serving informal workers:

TYPES OF FUNDING ALIGNED TO INITIATIVE GOALS:

COMMUNITY/SOCIAL INITIATIVES:
- Google.org Accelerator (tech for good)
- AWS Imagine Grant (nonprofits using cloud)
- SBI Foundation SBIF LEAP
- ImpactAI Foundry
- CSR funds from local corporations
  (align initiative impact to their CSR goals)

SKILL/LIVELIHOOD INITIATIVES:
- NSDC (National Skill Development Corporation)
- State skill development missions
- MUDRA loans for micro-enterprises
- Self Help Group linkage programs

EDUCATION INITIATIVES:
- NEP-aligned state education funds
- District education board partnerships
- Corporate CSR for education (most common)

PRINCIPLE FOR FUND ALIGNMENT:
Never seek funds that misalign with the initiative's purpose.
The fund's requirement shapes what you build.
Build what you believe, then find the fund that
believes the same thing.
Funds that require you to change your core purpose
are not funds — they are acquisitions.

The AI should help users:
1. Identify their initiative's core social impact
2. Match that impact to aligned funding sources
3. Frame the initiative in the fund's language
   without changing the initiative's substance
[/SECTION]

[SECTION: survival_to_drive_migration]
The practical path from caught-in-job to living-the-drive:

MONTH 1-3: CALCULATE AND STABILISE
- Calculate exact survival floor
- Confirm current income covers it
- Identify available bandwidth (time + energy)
- Do nothing else yet — just observe

MONTH 3-6: MINIMAL VIABLE INITIATIVE
- Start the smallest possible version of the drive
- $100 startup spirit: what can start today?
- No quitting, no major changes
- Test the core assumption:
  will anyone pay for / engage with this?

MONTH 6-12: FEEDBACK AND ITERATION
- Lean: what is the feedback saying?
- Systems: what is growing naturally?
- Habits: what keystone habit supports the drive?
- Measure: is drive income growing?

YEAR 1-2: THRESHOLD WATCHING
- When drive income reaches 30% of floor:
  note it, don't act yet
- When drive income reaches 60% of floor:
  begin planning the transition
- When drive income reaches 100% of floor:
  transition is now rational, not just emotional

YEAR 2+: INTEGRATION
- Drive is the livelihood
- Primary commitment exits naturally
- No dramatic quitting story needed
- The migration completed itself

NOTE: Timeline is illustrative, not prescriptive.
Some migrations happen in 6 months.
Some take 10 years.
The floor calculation is the only real measure —
not the calendar.
[/SECTION]
